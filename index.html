A must-read story

When the oil rush struck Pennsylvania in 1863, a prospector from Philadelphia decided to search for oil in his hometown rather than travel to Northwestern Pennsylvania.

He didn't strike oil but instead found something far more interesting--honey. He and his family took it from the source and transformed it into rich, golden mead, but the existence of the well and the origins of the drink would remain a family secret for the next 150 years.

Only in 2018 did his descendants finally introduce the delicious drink to the world; a drink made from honey, extracted from the family's secret well in Philadelphia.
